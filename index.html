Safety Tips for Pesticide Handling

Follow these essential safety measures to protect yourself and the environment.

Always wear gloves to prevent direct contact with pesticides.

Protect your respiratory system from harmful fumes and dust.

Prevent eye irritation or injury from pesticide splashes.

Do not eat, drink, or smoke while handling pesticides to avoid ingestion.

Clean yourself and your clothes thoroughly after using pesticides.

Pesticides

Pesticides are chemicals used to control pests in agriculture. While they improve crop yield, excessive use can harm humans, animals, and the environment. This project explains the main types of pesticides, safe handling, and preventive measures to minimize risks.

Fertilizers

Fertilizers provide essential nutrients for plants. Overuse can lead to soil degradation, water pollution, and imbalance in crop health. This project provides guidance on correct usage, eco-friendly alternatives, and methods to reduce negative impact.

How to Reduce Harm

  • Use pesticides and fertilizers only as recommended.
  • Follow safety guidelines and wear protective gear.
  • Prefer organic or bio-pesticides where possible.
  • Maintain crop rotation and natural pest control methods.
  • Proper disposal of chemical containers to prevent contamination.
